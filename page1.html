Tegucigalpa

Few cities have the colonial and modern concept that is seen in Tegucigalpa. Honduras. There are few cities in the world with contrasts that are seen in Tegucigalpa. This city is a cultural hub that will ensure that any tourist finds enjoyable activities to do. The first records of Tegucigalpa date back to 1560 when Spanish explorers decided to create a settlement in order to mine for silver in the surrounding areas. Tegucigalpa was an important mining center during the colonial era and this caused the economy to boom in the city. After Honduras’s independence from the Federal Republic of Central America, the city was declared the capital thanks to its contribution to the economy and its high population. Tegucigalpa is important for Honduras because of its high amounts of minerals like Silver which lead to increased amounts of trade and commerce. Honduran silver from Tegucigalpa has been exported to many countries in the region thanks to its low shipment prices. This causes the city to be vital for the Honduran economy.If you go to Tegucigalpa, you will mainly find lots of museums and city tours that teach you about Honduran culture and history. You can also visit the ancient colonial structures that are present in the city. All in all, you are guaranteed to find something interesting and fun to do whilst you stay in Tegucigalpa.

Lake Yojoa

In the depths of the Honduran jungle and just East of Tegucigalpa, you will find Honduras’s largest inland lake. This magnificent lake has entertainment and activities for the most adventurous or relaxed tourists. According to historians, the lake was formed with Volcanic activity thousands of years ago. This caused the lake to have fertile grounds surrounding it, this proved to be fantastic for plant and animal species to thrive around the lake. For centuries it has served as a fishing and hunting ground for indigenous people who created settlements around it. The lake is not only important for Honduras but to Central America in general because it has a large supply of natural minerals and resources which have been created from the volcanic minerals found surrounding the lake. Today, tourists go to the lake to enjoy some of the 400 species of birds and 800 plant species that have been identified in the lake and its surrounding areas. Some tourists also like to hire indigenous tour guides. This location can’t be missed on your next trip to Honduras, it is a natural beauty that can really give you amazing memories and experiences.

Comayagua

Comayagua is a municipality and a city in the Republic of Honduras. Comayagua was the capital before Tegucigalpa and is the current capital of the homonymous department located in the valley, also located in the central region of Honduras. It was founded in 1537 by the Spanish Captain General Alonso de Cáceres under the name "Villa de Santa María de la Nueva Valladolid de Comayagua". This city was the capital of Honduras from 1825 to 1880 until President Marco Aurelio Soto changed the country's capital. The colonial past of the city during the time of New Spain is evident in some of its old churches, the impressive Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception, colonial squares, museums, as well as republican civil architecture according to its historical evolution. Due to this, Comayagua is considered one of the wonders of Honduras and has become a national and international destination for tourism. This city has been very important over the years; it has been an important religious and political center in Honduras and Central America. The Comayagua Cathedral is the largest and most important colonial monument in the city. It is the largest temple going back to colonial times in the country. If you ever decide to visit this city, you should go and visit all of the colonial monuments, old churches, the cathedral, and museums that are so important to this city.

Guanaja Island

Have you ever heard of the Bay Islands? The Bay Islands are a group of islands off the coast of Honduras. And Guanaja Island is one of them. This island is about 70 kilometers from the north coast of Honduras. It has been described as the Venice of Honduras because of the waterways that run through it. The main town on this island is called Lower Cays, The other two main settlements on the island are called Mangrove Bight and Savannah Bight. There are also other smaller towns, like East End and North East Bight. The island has many mangroves and pine forests, and there are about 6,000 people on the island. The economy of this island is very basic. It depends on fishing, Shrimp Companies, also income that the island receives from tourism; hotels, restaurants, transportation, diving schools, etc. Living in Guanaja is very comfortable too it has hot water, schools, supermarkets, electricity, internet, and much more. The Bay Islands are very important for Honduras because of how much history and beauty they have and even though Guanaja Island is the least visited by tourists because of how difficult it is to get there, it’s still one of them. Guanaja island has a gorgeous ocean and it is perfect for snorkeling and diving, which you should try out if you ever have the opportunity to go. You can also go fishing and hiking and you can’t miss going to the beautiful waterfalls.

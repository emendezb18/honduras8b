Cangrejal River

If you head straight towards Honduras´s northern coast you are bound to cross your path with the Cangrejal River. Declared one of the 30 wonders of Honduras, the Cangrejal river has a variety of activities to ensure that you have a pleasant time in Honduras. The Cangrejal river has served as an entrance from the Caribbean into mainland Honduras and backs out into the Caribbean. It was one of the pillars of Spanish trade and supply transportation during the colonization of Honduras. The river goes through the city of La Ceiba and has made it grow exponentially over time as one of Honduras´s most important trade cities. The river is important for Honduras because it facilitates the country’s access to imported goods. The river´s location serves as a midpoint for the flow of goods throughout the region and allows Honduras to send and receive resources easily. Today the river is extremely important to the Honduran tourism industry as it provides numerous activities such as rafting, rock climbing, bird watching, and trekking. Most of the river goes through a lush valley filled with abundant plant and animal species. As a consequence, you can almost guarantee that anyone who has the chance to enjoy this river will have an amazing time gazing at a true wonder of nature.

Gulf of Honduras

Straight off the northern coast of Honduras you will encounter an ocean section like no other in Latin America. If you look under the surface you will have the privilege do take a glance at a colorful and mystic underwater world. The Gulf of Honduras is home to the Caribbean barrier reef and all of its marvelous species. The Gulf of Honduras was created 150 million years ago thanks to tectonic movement from the Central American plate. It extends 150km from La Ceiba, Honduras all the way to Dangriga, Belize. It is the 2nd largest coral reef in the world and is designated as a UNESCO Heritage site. The Gulf of Honduras brings around 100,000 to 200,000 tourists to the region each year. People from all over the world come to this location to have a chance to see the Caribbean reef’s unique plant and animal species. The Honduran tourism industry greatly benefits from this natural paradise which is vital for the economic and tourist growth of the country and region in general. If you have the chance to go to the Honduran Gulf you will be able to enjoy a handful of aquatic activities. Snorkeling, scuba diving, and fishing are just some of the excellent experiences the Honduran Gulf has to offer. You simply cannot miss learning about new animal species and a true natural pearl created by mother nature. If you’re lucky you might even get the chance to see Hammerhead sharks and Bottlenose dolphins. If you go to Honduras, make sure you get the chance to discover the secrets hidden under the gulf’s surface.
